RECONNECT-US Data Protection Policy

Purpose

This policy outlines how RECONNECT-US protects personal data and ensures compliance with the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018. It supports the privacy rights of clients, staff, volunteers, and partners.

1. Scope

This policy applies to all personal data held by RECONNECT-US, whether in digital or paper form, and to all individuals handling that data.

2. Data Protection Principles

  • Processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently
  • Collected for specified, legitimate purposes only
  • Limited to what is necessary
  • Accurate and kept up to date
  • Stored only as long as required
  • Protected with appropriate security and confidentiality

3. Lawful Basis for Processing

RECONNECT-US will identify and document lawful bases for all data processing activities, including:

  • Consent
  • Contractual necessity
  • Legal obligation
  • Legitimate interests (balanced with individual rights)

4. Data Subject Rights

Individuals have the right to:

  • Access their personal data
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion or restriction
  • Object to processing
  • Data portability where applicable
  • Withdraw consent at any time

Requests will be handled promptly within legal timeframes.

5. Data Collection and Storage

  • Data collected will be limited to what is necessary
  • Secure storage using passwords and encryption where possible
  • Paper records stored in locked locations
  • Access restricted to authorised personnel only

6. Data Sharing

  • Data will not be shared without consent unless legally required
  • Secure methods and agreements will be used when sharing data

7. Data Breaches

  • All breaches must be reported immediately
  • Management will assess and contain the breach
  • ICO and individuals will be notified if required

8. Training and Awareness

  • All staff and volunteers receive training at induction
  • Ongoing training ensures awareness of responsibilities

9. Retention and Disposal

  • Data retained only as long as necessary
  • Secure disposal methods used (e.g. shredding, secure deletion)

10. Review

This policy will be reviewed annually or when legislation or organisational needs change.
