RECONNECT-US Prevent Policy

1. Purpose of This Policy

This policy outlines how RECONNECT-US works to prevent individuals from being drawn into terrorism, extremism, or harmful ideologies.

We support people on probation, individuals leaving prison, and the wider community. Some may be vulnerable or at risk of exploitation. This policy demonstrates our commitment to safeguarding and public safety.

2. Our Commitment

  • Comply with the UK Prevent Duty (Counter-Terrorism and Security Act 2015).
  • Train staff and volunteers to recognise signs of radicalisation.
  • Work with police, probation, local authorities, and safeguarding boards.
  • Protect freedom of speech while preventing extremist influence.
  • Prioritise the safety and wellbeing of all.

3. Scope

  • All RECONNECT-US staff, volunteers, and contractors
  • Partner organisations
  • All service users

4. Understanding Radicalisation

Radicalisation is the process by which individuals come to support extremist ideologies or actions, sometimes leading to terrorism.

  • Political or religious extremism
  • Hate-based ideologies
  • Online radicalisation or conspiracy content

5. Indicators of Risk

  • Language: Extreme or hateful views
  • Behaviour: Withdrawal or sudden changes
  • Online activity: Engagement with extremist content
  • Associations: Links to radical groups
  • Symbols: Display of extremist imagery

6. Response Procedure

  1. Remain calm and do not confront the individual.
  2. Report concerns immediately to the Prevent Lead or safeguarding officer.
  3. The Prevent Lead will:
    • Record and assess the concern
    • Determine risk level
    • Contact Prevent/Channel where appropriate
  4. Work with relevant agencies to support the individual safely.

7. Confidentiality

Information will be shared on a need-to-know basis in line with safeguarding procedures. Where risk is identified, information may be shared without consent.

8. Training

  • Mandatory Prevent training at induction
  • Refresher training every two years or when legislation changes
  • Focus on recognising and reporting concerns

9. Freedom of Expression

  • Encourage open discussion
  • Do not permit extremist views
  • Challenge hate or discrimination appropriately
  • Maintain safe and respectful environments

10. Monitoring and Review

  • Annual policy review
  • Updated in line with legislation and best practice
  • Feedback from staff, volunteers, and service users included
