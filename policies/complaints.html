RECONNECT-US Complaints Policy

1. Purpose

RECONNECT-US values feedback from service users, staff, and partners. We understand that sometimes things may go wrong, and individuals may be unhappy with the service they receive. This policy explains how complaints are made, handled, and resolved.

2. What is a Complaint?

A complaint is when someone tells us they are unhappy with:

  • The way they have been treated by RECONNECT-US staff or volunteers
  • The quality of service provided
  • A decision made by RECONNECT-US
  • Something that feels unfair, unsafe, or not in line with our policies

A complaint is not:

  • A general comment or suggestion
  • A request for information
  • An appeal against a formal decision (appeals follow a separate process)

3. Who Can Make a Complaint?

  • Any RECONNECT-US service user
  • A family member, friend, or advocate acting on behalf of a service user
  • Staff, volunteers, or partner organisations

4. How to Make a Complaint

Complaints can be made in several ways:

  • In writing using the RECONNECT-US Complaint Form
  • By email: reconnectus@outlook.com
  • By phone: [Insert number]
  • In person to a staff member

If you need help completing a complaint form, RECONNECT-US staff will support you.

5. How We Handle Complaints

  1. Acknowledge the complaint within 5 working days
  2. Investigate fairly, involving relevant individuals
  3. Provide a response within 20 working days
  4. If delays occur, provide an updated response timeframe

6. Our Approach

  • Listen carefully and take concerns seriously
  • Maintain confidentiality unless there is a risk of harm
  • Review all relevant information before making decisions
  • Resolve issues fairly and promptly
  • Use feedback to improve services

7. If You Are Not Satisfied

If you are not satisfied with the outcome, you may request a review by a senior manager not involved in the original decision.

If concerns remain, you may escalate to an independent body such as:

  • A regulatory authority
  • An ombudsman
  • A legal adviser

8. Confidentiality

All complaints will be handled in line with the RECONNECT-US Confidentiality Policy. Information will only be shared where necessary to resolve the complaint.

9. Records

A record of all complaints, actions taken, and outcomes will be maintained. This supports monitoring, accountability, and service improvement.

10. Review

This policy will be reviewed annually to ensure it remains up to date and aligned with best practice.
