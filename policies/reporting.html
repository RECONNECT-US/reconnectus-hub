RECONNECT-US Reporting Online Safety Issues Policy

1. Purpose

This policy sets out how clients, staff, and volunteers at RECONNECT-US can report online safety concerns. It ensures that issues are dealt with promptly, fairly, and effectively, keeping everyone safe while using digital tools and services.

2. Scope

  • All RECONNECT-US staff, volunteers, and clients
  • Any devices or online platforms used for RECONNECT-US activities
  • Any online safety issue encountered during RECONNECT-US sessions or linked to the organisation’s services

3. What to Report

Concerns that should be reported include, but are not limited to:

  • Scams or phishing attempts, including emails, texts, pop-ups, and fake websites
  • Cyberbullying or harassment through social media, email, or messaging apps
  • Suspicious or unsafe links and downloads
  • Inappropriate, harmful, or illegal online content
  • Privacy or data protection concerns, such as personal details being shared without consent
  • Sextortion, exploitation, or grooming attempts
  • Technical issues that may compromise safety, such as hacked accounts

4. Reporting Procedure

  1. Immediate Action
    • If you feel unsafe or threatened, stop using the device or app immediately
    • Take screenshots or note details of the concern, if it is safe to do so
  2. Report to RECONNECT-US
    • Clients should report the issue to their session facilitator, support worker, or volunteer
    • Staff and volunteers should report directly to their line manager or safeguarding lead
  3. Logging the Concern
    • The concern must be recorded using the RECONNECT-US Online Safety Reporting Form, whether digital or paper
    • Include the date, time, type of issue, description, evidence if available, and action taken
  4. Escalation
    • Safeguarding concerns, including harassment, grooming, or exploitation, must be escalated to the Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) immediately
    • Data breaches must be reported to the Data Protection Officer (DPO) within 24 hours
    • Criminal or high-risk issues may be reported to the police or other external agencies

5. Response & Follow-Up

  • Reports will be acknowledged within 2 working days
  • Serious cases will be investigated immediately by the DSL and/or DPO
  • Clients and staff will be supported throughout the process
  • Where necessary, digital safety guidance will be provided to help prevent repeat issues

6. Confidentiality

All reports will be handled sensitively. Information will only be shared with relevant staff and external agencies when necessary for safety or legal reasons.

7. Responsibilities

  • Clients: Report any concerns promptly
  • Staff and Volunteers: Support clients in reporting issues and follow procedures
  • Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL): Oversee safeguarding-related online safety concerns
  • Data Protection Officer (DPO): Handle data protection and privacy-related concerns
  • Management: Ensure all staff and volunteers are trained in this policy

8. Review

This policy will be reviewed annually, or sooner if significant online safety developments occur.
