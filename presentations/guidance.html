Online Safety Training

RECONNECT-US Guidance

Policies and best practices for safe communication, responsible use of digital tools, and protecting staff, clients, and the wider community across RECONNECT-US services.

Purpose

  • Provide clear rules for safe communication
  • Support clients in using digital tools responsibly

Core Aim

  • Protect staff, clients, and the wider community
  • Set shared expectations for safer online behaviour

Communication Rules

Communication should stay respectful, professional, and inside approved channels so that everyone is safer and there is a clear record of support interactions.

Professional Conduct

  • Use respectful and professional language at all times
  • Staff and clients must not share personal contact details

Approved Channels

  • All communication through approved RECONNECT-US channels such as WhatsApp or email
  • No sharing of offensive or illegal content

WhatsApp & Messaging Guidelines

Messaging should support training and client safety, not blur professional boundaries.

Group Use

  • Group chats monitored by staff for safety
  • Messages should be relevant to support and training

Boundaries

  • No private messaging between staff and clients unless authorised
  • Inappropriate behaviour will be reported and acted on

Email & Online Meetings

Official accounts and approved meeting routes help keep communication secure, professional, and easier to manage.

Email Safety

  • Use RECONNECT-US email accounts where possible
  • Be cautious with links and attachments

Meeting Rules

  • Zoom or Teams meetings must be scheduled through official channels
  • Respect privacy and do not record unless agreed by all

Reporting Online Safety Issues

Problems should be reported early so risks can be handled quickly and escalated through the correct RECONNECT-US process.

What to Report

  • Report phishing or scam messages immediately
  • Inform staff if accounts are hacked or compromised

Escalation

  • Harassment, threats, or abuse online must be reported
  • There should be a clear escalation process within RECONNECT-US

Key Takeaways

Safe communication is a shared responsibility. Using approved channels and reporting concerns helps protect everyone involved.

What to Remember

  • Use only approved RECONNECT-US communication channels
  • Keep conversations respectful and professional

What to Do

  • Report any suspicious or harmful behaviour
  • Online safety is everyone’s responsibility

Support & Contacts

Use official support routes when you need further guidance or to report online crime and safety concerns.
RECONNECT-US IT Safety — internal support for safer communication and digital practice.
Get Safe Online — www.getsafeonline.org for further guidance.
Police Non-Emergency — 101 for reporting online crime or concerns.
Emergency Contact — 999 in emergencies where there is immediate risk.
