1. Purpose of this Policy

RECONNECT-US works with people on probation and those leaving long prison sentences. Safeguarding means keeping everyone safe, our staff, our service users and the organisations we work with, including the probation service. This policy explains how we will protect people from harm, keep information safe, and respond if there are concerns.

2. Our Commitment

  • Protecting the safety, dignity and wellbeing of all people involved in our work.
  • Creating an environment where staff, service users and partners feel safe, respected, and supported.
  • Taking quick and proper action if a safeguarding concern is raised.

3. Who This Policy Covers

  • All RECONNECT-US staff, whether paid or volunteer.
  • All service users who take part in our activities.
  • Any partner organisations, including the probation service, when working with us.

4. Safeguarding Principles

  • Respect – We treat everyone with fairness, dignity, and understanding.
  • Safety First – We act to prevent harm before it happens.
  • Clear Boundaries – We keep professional boundaries at all times.
  • Confidentiality – We protect private information, only sharing it when needed to keep people safe.
  • Transparency – We are open and honest about how we work.

5. Safeguarding for Staff

  • Make sure staff have the training they need to spot and deal with safeguarding issues.
  • Provide support and supervision to reduce stress and prevent burnout.
  • Offer a safe working environment, including risk assessments for in-person and online work.
  • Make sure staff can raise concerns without fear of punishment.

6. Safeguarding for Service Users

  • Welcome all service users in a safe and respectful way.
  • Protect them from abuse, discrimination, harassment, or exploitation.
  • Make sure our spaces are physically safe and free from risks.
  • Give clear information about our rules and what behaviour is acceptable.
  • Support service users to report any concerns about their own safety or the safety of others.

7. Safeguarding for the Probation Service and Other Partners

  • Work in a way that supports the safety and reputation of the probation service.
  • Share safeguarding concerns quickly with probation officers when needed.
  • Keep to all agreed safeguarding and data-protection requirements.
  • Make sure our work does not put the probation service, its staff, or its service users at risk.

8. Reporting Concerns

  • Report it to the RECONNECT-US Safeguarding Lead as soon as possible.
  • If there is an immediate danger, call the police on 999.
  • The Safeguarding Lead will record the concern and take the right action, including contacting probation officers or other agencies when needed.

9. Review of Policy

This policy will be reviewed once a year, or sooner if there are changes in law, guidance, or our services.
